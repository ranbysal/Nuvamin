(01)

Identity

Every lot is confirmed by high-resolution mass spectrometry against the reference sequence. The vial contains the molecule on the label — provably, not presumably.

(02)

Purity

HPLC purity verified on every batch, with the chromatogram published. Our release floor is 98%; most lots clear 99%. Below spec never ships — it is destroyed.

(03)

Quantity

A 10 mg vial should contain 10 mg. We verify net peptide content by quantitative amino-acid analysis, not by weighing the salt and hoping.

(04)

Stability

Lyophilised under vacuum, sealed under nitrogen, stored and shipped cold-chain at −20 °C. Degradation is a supply-chain choice. We choose otherwise.

03 — Inside every vial

The verification
standard

Four checks stand between synthesis and your bench. Each one is performed by an independent laboratory, and each one is published. Boring, strict, non-negotiable.

Our standards →

HPLC–MS

Identity & purity

Third-party high-performance liquid chromatography and mass spectrometry on every production lot. The chromatogram and spectrum ship with the vial and live in the batch archive.

Net content

The mg on the label

Quantitative amino-acid analysis confirms actual peptide mass per vial — not gross weight inflated by counter-ions and moisture. Under-filled lots do not release.

Sterility screen

Endotoxin & bioburden

Each lot is screened for endotoxin (LAL) and microbial contamination before release, with results stated on the certificate rather than implied by the word “sterile”.

Published CoA

Look your lot up

Every certificate of analysis is archived online for five years, searchable by lot number. If a supplier won’t show you the paper, you already have your answer.
