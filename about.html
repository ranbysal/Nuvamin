The company

We started Nuvamin
because the vial
should match the paper.

The research-chemical market runs on trust it hasn’t earned: unlabelled purity, absent certificates, powders that are whatever the last synthesis happened to yield. Nuvamin is an argument that research materials can be sold the way research is done — measured, documented, and open to scrutiny.

Our standards

Six rules,
zero exceptions

(01)

Verified identity

Every lot is confirmed by high-resolution mass spectrometry against the reference sequence before it is assigned a catalogue number. Unconfirmed material never becomes a product.

(02)

Published purity

HPLC purity is measured on every batch and the chromatogram is published. Our release floor is 98%. Anything below it is quarantined and destroyed, not discounted.

(03)

Honest quantity

Net peptide content is confirmed by quantitative amino-acid analysis, so a 10 mg vial holds 10 mg of peptide — not gross weight padded with salt and water.

(04)

Independent laboratories

Testing is performed by accredited third-party labs with no stake in the result. Certificates ship in the box and remain searchable online by lot number for five years.

(05)

Intact cold chain

Lyophilised under vacuum, sealed under nitrogen, dispatched on dry ice. Stability is protected from our freezer to your bench, and the packaging proves it on arrival.

(06)

Research use only

We sell to qualified researchers and institutions for laboratory use. We make no therapeutic claims, and we would rather lose a sale than pretend these are anything they are not.

The process

From synthesis
to certificate

Each compound moves through four gates. Solid-phase synthesis to the reference sequence; purification and lyophilisation; independent HPLC–MS and content analysis; release only when every number lands inside spec. Material that fails any gate does not proceed — it is documented and destroyed.

Gate 1

Synthesise

Solid-phase peptide synthesis to the published sequence, using reagents sourced against a written specification.

Gate 2

Purify

Preparative HPLC purification, then lyophilisation under vacuum and sealing under nitrogen to protect stability.

Gate 3

Verify

An independent laboratory confirms identity, purity, net content, endotoxin and bioburden. Results become the certificate.

Gate 4

Release

Only in-spec lots are labelled, archived and shipped cold-chain. The lot number ties the vial to its paperwork for five years.
